## Clinical History and Background

### Identifying Information

Jose is a male patient in his late 40s to early 50s who presented for outpatient psychiatric evaluation. He was referred by his primary care physician after reporting persistent low mood and difficulty functioning at work over the preceding six months.

### Reason for Consultation

The patient reports a gradual onset of depressed mood, anhedonia, and significant fatigue beginning approximately six months ago. He describes feeling persistently sad and empty, with markedly diminished interest in activities he previously enjoyed, including exercise and social outings with friends. He endorses difficulty concentrating at work, describing his cognitive function as foggy and slowed. Sleep is disturbed with frequent early-morning awakening and difficulty returning to sleep. Appetite has decreased, with an unintentional weight loss of approximately 8 pounds over four months. He denies active suicidal ideation but acknowledges passive thoughts about not wanting to wake up on particularly difficult mornings. He reports increased irritability at home, which has strained his relationship with his spouse.

### Previous Diagnoses and Treatment History

- **Major Depressive Disorder, recurrent, moderate** -- diagnosed approximately 8 years ago by a previous psychiatrist. He completed a course of sertraline 100 mg daily at that time with good response and remained stable for several years before self-discontinuing.
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder** -- noted in prior records, with chronic worry and muscle tension that predated the depressive episodes.
- No prior psychiatric hospitalizations.
- Previous trial of escitalopram approximately 5 years ago was discontinued due to gastrointestinal side effects.
- Brief course of supportive psychotherapy 4 years ago, attended 8 sessions, reported as moderately helpful.

### Current Medication

- Sertraline 50 mg daily -- recently restarted by primary care physician 3 weeks ago. The patient reports no significant improvement yet and endorses mild nausea that has been improving.
- No other psychotropic medications.
- Denies use of over-the-counter supplements or herbal remedies.
- No current benzodiazepine use, though he reports occasional past use of lorazepam prescribed by a previous provider.

### Medical History

- Hypertension, diagnosed 3 years ago, managed with lisinopril 10 mg daily.
- Mild hyperlipidemia, managed with diet.
- No history of head trauma, seizures, or neurological conditions.
- No known thyroid dysfunction, though recent labs are pending.

### Substance Use

- Alcohol: reports drinking 2 to 3 beers on weekend evenings, denies daily use or binge drinking patterns.
- Tobacco: none.
- Caffeine: 3 to 4 cups of coffee daily.
- Denies use of cannabis, opioids, stimulants, or other illicit substances.
- No history of substance use disorder treatment.

### Risk Factors

- **Family history**: Mother has history of recurrent depression treated with medication. Paternal uncle died by suicide at age 52. No known family history of bipolar disorder or psychotic illness.
- **Psychosocial stressors**: Significant work-related stress over the past year following a corporate restructuring and increased workload. Reports feeling undervalued and concerned about job security.
- **Personality and coping style**: Tends toward perfectionism and self-criticism. Difficulty delegating tasks and a pattern of overcommitting professionally, leading to cycles of burnout.
- **Protective factors**: Stable marriage of 22 years, two adult children with whom he maintains close relationships, steady employment, no legal or financial problems, insight into his condition, and willingness to engage in treatment.

### Psychosocial Context

The patient was born and raised in a bicultural household. He is the eldest of three siblings and describes his childhood as generally stable, though he notes that his mother struggled with extended periods of low mood during his adolescence. He completed a bachelor's degree and has worked in middle management for the same company for over 15 years. His marriage is described as largely supportive, though his spouse has expressed frustration with his emotional withdrawal and irritability over recent months. He identifies his faith community as a source of support but reports he has withdrawn from regular attendance during this depressive episode. Financial resources are adequate, and he has good health insurance coverage. He expresses motivation to feel better and willingness to consider both medication management and psychotherapy.

### Mental Status Examination

The patient presented as a well-groomed male appearing his stated age. Eye contact was intermittent. Speech was of normal rate and rhythm but low in volume and prosody. Mood was described as low and frustrated. Affect was constricted and congruent with mood. Thought process was linear and goal-directed. Thought content was notable for negative self-appraisal but no delusions, hallucinations, or ideas of reference. He denied current suicidal intent, plan, or means. Cognition appeared grossly intact with intact attention and concentration during the interview. Insight was fair. Judgment was intact.

### Preliminary Formulation

Jose presents with a recurrent major depressive episode of moderate severity in the context of chronic workplace stress and longstanding maladaptive perfectionism. Contributing factors include a significant family history of mood disorder and suicide, recent medication discontinuation, and withdrawal from previously protective social and spiritual supports. The restarted sertraline trial is at a sub-therapeutic dose for maintenance and has not yet had adequate time for full effect. A combined approach of medication optimization and evidence-based psychotherapy targeting cognitive patterns and behavioral activation is indicated.
