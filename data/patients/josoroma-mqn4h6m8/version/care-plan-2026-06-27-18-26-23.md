## Care Plan -- josoroma (josoroma-mqn4h6m8)

### Treatment Goals

1. Reduce depressive symptom severity from **moderately severe (avg 2.4)** to **mild (avg below 1.5)** on the Level 1 Cross-Cutting measure within 12 weeks.
2. Decrease anxiety-related impairment by achieving a **GAD-7 score below 10** (moderate to mild threshold) within 8 weeks.
3. Reduce irritability and anger reactivity by lowering the irritability domain score from **2 (moderate) to 1 (mild)** on the Level 1 measure within 10 weeks.
4. Improve sleep onset and maintenance, measured by patient self-report of **3 or fewer disrupted nights per week** and discontinuation of OTC sleep aid by week 8.
5. Address somatic symptom distress by reducing the Clinician-Rated Somatic Severity from **severe (4/4) to mild (2/4)** within 12 weeks.

### Recommended Interventions

- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** -- weekly individual sessions, 50 minutes each. Focus on cognitive restructuring for depressive rumination, behavioral activation to counter anhedonia, and exposure-based techniques for anxiety.
- **Anger Management Module** -- integrated into CBT sessions starting week 3. Include trigger identification, arousal reduction techniques, and cognitive reframing of anger-provoking situations.
- **Sleep Hygiene Protocol** -- structured intervention delivered in session 2. Elements: consistent sleep-wake schedule, stimulus control, wind-down routine, and sleep diary tracking.
- **Relaxation Training** -- progressive muscle relaxation and diaphragmatic breathing introduced at session 4. Patient to practice daily with audio guidance.
- **Psychoeducation** -- depression and anxiety cycle, somatic symptom connection, and treatment rationale delivered across sessions 1 and 2.
- **Psychiatric Consultation Trigger** -- if PHQ-9 reduction is less than 25% from baseline by week 4, refer for medication evaluation. Prior SSRI trial with partial response noted; consider alternative class or augmentation.

### Follow-up Plan

- **PHQ-9** -- administer every 2 weeks, starting at intake. Track total score and item 9 (suicidality) at every administration.
- **GAD-7** -- administer every 2 weeks, alternating with PHQ-9 weeks or combined when clinically indicated.
- **Sleep Diary** -- weekly review in session. Track sleep onset latency, total sleep time, awakenings, and OTC sleep aid use.
- **Anger Log** -- weekly review. Document triggers, intensity (1-10), and coping strategy used.
- **Level 1 Cross-Cutting Reassessment** -- full measure at week 8 to evaluate domain-level change.
- **Clinician-Rated Somatic Severity** -- re-administer at week 8 alongside Level 1.
- **Comprehensive Treatment Review** -- week 12. Evaluate goal attainment, reassess diagnosis, and determine continuation versus transition to maintenance.

### Discharge Criteria

- PHQ-9 score at or below 9 (mild range) sustained across two consecutive biweekly administrations
- GAD-7 score at or below 9 (mild range) on two consecutive administrations
- Level 1 Cross-Cutting domain scores all at or below 2 (mild), with average below 1.5
- Clinician-Rated Somatic Severity at or below 2 (mild)
- Patient demonstrates independent use of at least 3 coping strategies as documented in anger logs and session notes
- Functional improvement reported in at least two domains: occupational, social, family, or self-care
- Mutual agreement between patient and clinician that treatment goals have been met and discharge is appropriate

### Risk Mitigation

- **Suicide Risk Screening** -- PHQ-9 item 9 assessed at every session. Any score of 2 or higher triggers full Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) administration same day.
- **Safety Plan** -- develop collaboratively at session 2. Include warning signs, internal coping strategies, social contacts, professional resources, and means restriction. Review and update every 4 weeks or after any item-9 score increase.
- **Crisis Protocol** -- patient provided with 24/7 crisis line number, nearest emergency department directions, and emergency contact authorization. Documented in chart and reviewed at session 1.
- **Substance Use Screening** -- AUDIT-C and DAST-10 administered at intake and every 8 weeks. Any positive screen triggers full assessment and integrated treatment planning.
- **Treatment Non-Response Flag** -- if PHQ-9 reduction is less than 25% at week 6 review, initiate treatment plan modification: increase session frequency, add group therapy referral, or expedite psychiatric consultation.
- **Missed Session Protocol** -- one missed session: outreach call within 24 hours. Two consecutive missed sessions without contact: welfare check per emergency contact authorization. Document all outreach attempts.
