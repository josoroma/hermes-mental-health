## Reason for Consultation

[Patient] presents with a two-year history of worsening anxiety symptoms and increasing difficulty managing daily responsibilities. Primary complaints include persistent worry across multiple life domains, restlessness, difficulty concentrating, and disrupted sleep — specifically early-morning awakening with inability to return to sleep.

Accompanying somatic complaints include chronic muscle tension, tension-type headaches occurring 3-4 times per week, and gastrointestinal distress described as non-specific abdominal discomfort with intermittent nausea. [Patient] also reports a notable increase in irritability over the past 12 months, with several episodes of verbal outbursts at work that prompted a formal performance review — the precipitating event for seeking care.

Secondary concerns include low mood, diminished interest in previously enjoyed activities (hobbies, social gatherings), and low energy, though these are described as less distressing than the anxiety symptoms.
