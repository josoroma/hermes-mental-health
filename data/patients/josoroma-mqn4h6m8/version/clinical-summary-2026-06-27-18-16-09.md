## Clinical Impressions

The assessment profile suggests a clinical picture characterized by prominent anxiety and somatic distress, with a secondary component of behavioral dysregulation manifesting as impulsivity or elevated risk-taking. The combination of moderate-level anxiety and severe clinician-rated somatic severity points to a possible somatic symptom disorder or an anxiety disorder with significant somatic features.

The mild elevations in anhedonia and irritability, while not independently severe, contribute to a broader picture of emotional dysregulation. The presence of impulsive or risky behavior at moderate frequency raises concern for possible hypomanic or disinhibited features and warrants further diagnostic clarification.

Notably, the Level 1 measure yielded a moderately severe global rating despite individual items clustering in the mild-to-moderate range, reflecting the cumulative impact of symptoms spanning multiple domains rather than extreme elevation in any single domain.

The substantial gap between the patient's self-reported somatic complaints (mild, item-level score of 2) and the clinician's severity rating (severe, Level 4) suggests that the clinician may be observing functional impairment, distress, or somatic preoccupation that exceeds what the single self-report item captures. This warrants exploration in the clinical interview.

## Recommendations

- Conduct a focused clinical interview to clarify the nature and duration of impulsive or risky behaviors, with specific attention to possible hypomanic or manic episodes
- Administer a Level 2 anxiety measure (e.g., PROMIS Anxiety or GAD-7) to further characterize the moderate anxiety elevation
- Explore the discrepancy between self-reported somatic symptoms and clinician-rated somatic severity through collateral history and functional assessment
- Consider a Level 2 somatic symptom measure for deeper characterization of the somatic complaint profile
- Screen for substance use, as it may contribute to both impulsive behavior and somatic presentations
- Repeat the Level 1 Cross-Cutting Symptom Measure at 4 to 6 weeks to monitor symptom trajectory and treatment response
- Assess for suicidal ideation directly, given the presence of irritability and impulsivity, which can elevate risk
