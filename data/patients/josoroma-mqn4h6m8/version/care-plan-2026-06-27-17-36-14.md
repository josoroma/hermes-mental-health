### Recommended Interventions

**Primary Modality: Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for Somatic Symptom Disorder**

- Frequency: Weekly individual sessions, 50 minutes
- Duration: 12–16 sessions initially, with formal progress review at session 8 to determine continuation vs. taper
- Provider: Licensed clinical psychologist or masters-level therapist with specific training in CBT for somatic symptom disorders

**Core CBT Components (sequenced by phase):**

*Phase 1 — Engagement & Psychoeducation (Sessions 1–3)*
1. Validation of the patient's somatic experience — explicitly affirm that symptoms are real and disabling
2. Psychoeducation on the mind-body connection: autonomic nervous system arousal, the stress-symptom cycle, and central sensitization
3. Collaborative development of a biopsychosocial formulation — introduce the cognitive-behavioral model of somatic symptoms without implying symptoms are imaginary
4. Introduction to self-monitoring: daily symptom log capturing intensity (0–10), context, thoughts, emotional state, and behavioral response

*Phase 2 — Cognitive Restructuring & Behavioral Experiments (Sessions 4–10)*
5. Cognitive restructuring targeting catastrophic misinterpretation of bodily sensations — identify automatic thoughts, evaluate evidence, generate balanced alternatives
6. Behavioral experiments to test illness-related predictions (e.g., "If I exercise, I will collapse" → graded activity to test the prediction)
7. Attention training to reduce hypervigilance toward bodily cues — interoceptive exposure exercises, sensory grounding, attention shifting
8. Graded activity scheduling with pacing to break the boom-bust cycle — establish consistent baseline activity level and incrementally build tolerance

*Phase 3 — Consolidation & Relapse Prevention (Sessions 11–16)*
9. Interoceptive exposure and symptom tolerance training — deliberately induce benign somatic sensations (e.g., hyperventilation, spinning) to decouple sensation from catastrophic interpretation
10. Relapse prevention planning: identify early warning signs, high-risk situations, and a written coping plan
11. Graduated step-down: weekly sessions → biweekly (×4) → monthly booster (×3) before discharge

**Adjunctive Interventions (as indicated by confirmatory assessments):**

- Behavioral activation for depressive symptoms — if PHQ-9 ≥10
- Exposure-based work (worry exposure, situational exposure) for anxiety/avoidance — if GAD-7 ≥10
- Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) or Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT) for general arousal regulation and decentering from somatic preoccupation
- Referral to psychiatry for medication evaluation — if moderate-to-severe depression (PHQ-9 ≥15) or anxiety (GAD-7 ≥15) confirmed

**Medical Coordination:**

- Establish formal communication with primary care provider (PCP) via written coordination letter
- Recommend single-point-of-contact medical management model — one designated PCP to reduce fragmented care and unnecessary investigations
- PCP letter to include: diagnosis, rationale for conservative diagnostic stewardship, request that new physical complaints route through therapist for collaborative triage before additional testing
- Schedule regular care coordination updates between therapist and PCP every 4–6 weeks
