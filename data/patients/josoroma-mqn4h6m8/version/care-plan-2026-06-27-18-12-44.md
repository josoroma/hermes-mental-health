## Treatment Goals

1. **Reduce depressive symptoms** — Decrease Level 1 depression domain score from 2 (mild) to 1 (none/slight) within 8 weeks as measured by repeat administration
2. **Manage anxiety symptoms** — Reduce Level 1 anxiety domain score from 3 (moderate) to 1 (none/slight) within 8 weeks; patient will report fewer than 2 days per week of clinically significant anxious distress
3. **Stabilize irritability and anger** — Reduce irritability/anger domain score from 3 (moderate) to 1 (none/slight) within 10 weeks; patient will demonstrate 2+ adaptive coping strategies for anger triggers
4. **Improve sleep quality** — Reduce sleep disturbance domain score from 2 (mild) to 1 (none/slight) within 6 weeks; patient will report consistent sleep onset within 30 minutes and fewer than 2 nocturnal awakenings per night
5. **Address somatic complaints** — Reduce somatic symptom domain score from 2 (mild) to 1 (none/slight) within 8 weeks; patient will identify and articulate the connection between emotional distress and physical symptoms in at least 2 specific contexts

---

## Recommended Interventions

- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** — Weekly individual sessions, 50 minutes, focused on cognitive restructuring and behavioral activation for depression and anxiety
- **Anger management module** — Integrate CBT-adjacent anger management techniques into sessions; introduce the STOP technique (Stop, Take a breath, Observe, Proceed) and thought-stopping for irritability triggers
- **Sleep hygiene intervention** — Structured sleep hygiene protocol: consistent sleep/wake schedule, stimulus control, 30-minute wind-down routine, and sleep diary tracking
- **Relaxation training** — Progressive muscle relaxation and diaphragmatic breathing exercises to address somatic symptoms and reduce physiological arousal
- **Psychoeducation** — Provide education on the mind-body connection, the anxiety-somatic symptom cycle, and the role of avoidance in maintaining symptoms
- **Psychiatric consultation** — Refer for medication evaluation if symptom reduction is less than 25% after 4 weeks of CBT; consider SSRI for depression/anxiety comorbidity

## Follow-up Plan

- **PHQ-9** — Administer every 2 weeks; next due week of July 11, 2026
- **GAD-7** — Administer every 2 weeks; next due week of July 11, 2026
- **Level 1 Cross-Cutting Symptom Measure** — Re-administer at week 8 (week of August 22, 2026) for full domain reassessment
- **Sleep diary review** — Review weekly during individual sessions; assess adherence to sleep hygiene protocol
- **Anger/irritability log** — Patient to maintain daily log of anger episodes (trigger, intensity 1-10, coping strategy used); review weekly
- **Clinical progress review** — Comprehensive treatment plan review at week 12; reassess goals, modify interventions as indicated

---

## Discharge Criteria

1. All five treatment goal domain scores reduced to 1 (none/slight) on the Level 1 Cross-Cutting measure
2. PHQ-9 score below 5 and GAD-7 score below 5 on two consecutive administrations
3. Patient demonstrates independent use of 3+ adaptive coping strategies without prompting
4. Patient self-reports sustained functional improvement across occupational and interpersonal domains for 4+ consecutive weeks
5. Sleep diary shows consistent sleep onset latency under 30 minutes and total sleep time of 7+ hours for 3+ consecutive weeks
6. No anger episodes rated above 5/10 intensity for 4+ consecutive weeks
7. Patient and clinician agree that treatment goals have been met and that the therapeutic relationship is no longer medically necessary

## Risk Mitigation

- **Suicide risk screening** — Administer PHQ-9 item 9 at every session; score of 2 or higher triggers full Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) assessment and safety planning
- **Safety plan** — Develop and document a collaborative safety plan including: warning signs, internal coping strategies, social contacts, professional emergency contacts, and means restriction; review and update every 4 weeks
- **Crisis protocol** — Patient provided with 24/7 crisis line number and nearest emergency department address; documented in patient file with acknowledgment of receipt
- **Substance use monitoring** — Screen for alcohol and substance use at intake and every 8 weeks; any positive screen triggers referral for substance use evaluation
- **Treatment non-response flag** — If symptom reduction is less than 25% on any domain by week 6, trigger treatment plan review, consider psychiatric consultation for medication, and assess for complicating factors
- **Missed session protocol** — Two consecutive missed or canceled sessions without 24-hour notice triggers outreach call; three missed sessions without contact triggers wellness check per clinic policy
